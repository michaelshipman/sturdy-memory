Othello

Michael Shipman Apr 20 2021-04-20T01:00:00-04:00
Apr 20 2021-04-20T15:15:36-04:00 14 min

Act 1

Scene 1

  • begins on a street in Venice
  • Roderigo and Iago are arguing
    • we learn that Roderigo has been paying Iago to help him pursue Desdemona
    • Roderigo is angry that Desdemona has married Othello
  • Iago hates Othello because he promoted Cassio over him
  • They decide to tell Desdemona’s father Brabantio about the secret marriage in order to rile him up and cause trouble for Othello
  • Brabantio doesn’t believe them at first but Iago through the use of vulgar language convinces Brabantio
  • Brabantio decries the use of magic charms to woo his daughter

Scene 2

  • Iago goes to Othello to warn him that Brabantio is coming and is angry
  • Cassio along with some messengers arrives to tell Othello that the Duke has sent for Othello for something to do with Cyprus
  • Brabantio then arrives and orders his men to attackOthello
  • Othello tells them that the Duke has summoned him and Brabantio decides to bring Othello to the Duke for judgment

Scene 3

  • reading about turkey invading Cyprus, blah blah blah emergency
  • the gang shows up
    • duke addresses Othello first
    • so Brabantio tattles
  • Duke takes accusation seriously and then Othello monologues
  • Brabantio keeps talking about how its unnatural for Desdemona to love Othello, projecting his racism onto his daughter
  • Duke wants proof
  • Othello monologues about how they fell in love and the lack of magic
    • turns out Othello really good at speaking
  • Desdemona shows up and confesses true love
    • Brabantio heckin angry
    • Duke is like “ok stop whining Brabantio”
  • The Duke sends Othello to support Cyprus against an incoming Turkish force

  • “Look to her, Moor, if thou hast eyes to see. She has deceived her father, and may thee.” - Brabantio with some mega foreshadowing

  • Everyone leaves except for Iago and Roderigo
    • Roderigo sad that Desdemona is surely lost to him
    • says he’s gonna drown himself
    • Iago mocks him for that
    • Iago brainwashes Roderigo into saving up a bunch of money to woo Desdemona but actually for Iago to use and steal essentially
  • Iago then delivers first soliloquy
    • declares hatred for Othello and reveals his suspicion that Othello may have slept with his wife (Emilia) while also saying he doubts its true
    • he says that he plans to cheat Roderigo of his money and convince Othello that Desdemona cheated on him with Cassio
    • plans to use Othello’s honesty against him

Act 2

Scene 1

  • Starts off with Montano talking about the battle with two gentlemen
    • Turks lost and Venetians won
    • bad weather and choppy conditions aided the Venetians
  • Othello got lost at sea in a storm and separated from Cassio
  • Montano wonders if Othello is married
  • Suffice to say Cassio has no romantic interest in Desdemona and is happy with them together, he speaks of her beauty but not in a romantic way
  • Iago is kinda mean to his wife
  • Desdemona challenges Iago to roast her
  • Othello shows up and is happy to see Desdemona, Iago has an aside where he basically says “Imma ruin this lol” on line 218
  • Iago tells Roderigo that Desdemona is in love with Cassio and convinces him (step 4 and 5 of his master plan)
    • Brainwashes provoking Cassio to a fight, this will hopefully remove Cassio as lieutenant for Iago (get him his position?) and Roderigo thinks it will get him closer to Desdemona
    • Also removes one of Othello’s supporters

Scene 2

  • Basically some exposition, time passes, there’s a party now which Iago will surely leverage

Scene 3

  • Othello out of play to have some wifey time
  • Iago starts talking about Othello and Desdemona’s love life
    • Cassio remains respectful
  • Iago tries to get Cassio drunk even though he doesn’t want to
  • They get incredibly drunk as evidenced by Iago’s singing
    • this includes Cassio…uh oh
  • Iago tells Montano that Cassio is a drunk
    • sows doubt in Cassio’s capabilities
  • Roderigo goes to pick a fight with Cassio
    • Cassio hits Roderigo
    • Roderigo goes and tattles on Cassio (by ringing a bell) so that everyone sees him drunk
  • Othello shows up and is annoyed about the kerfuffle
    • turns to his most trusted friend Iago to learn what happened
    • trying to figure out what caused the fight, unwittingly asking the source himself
    • Iago says it was Cassio and Othello promptly fires Cassio

      “Making it light to Cassio.-Cassio, I love thee, but nevermore be officer of mine.” - Othello (264-265)

  • Iago suggests that Cassio go talk to Desdemona to plea for his role back as he implies that Othello will listen to whatever she says
  • Soliloquy #3 happens
  • Roderigo promptly informs us hes run out of money, and been beaten up by Cassio, wants to leave to go home
    • but Iago being tricky and such convinces him to stay
  • Iago then outlines the rest of his plot
    • tells Emilia to talk to Desdemona on Cassio’s behalf
    • hopefully ends up making it look like they have a mutual relationship

Soliloquy #3

^e8e93a

  • Shows Iago convincing himself that he isn’t at fault
    • Questions if he’s the villain but argues that he’s giving honest advice and that this could actually work out for Cassio
    • Then says that the worst devils will come off as godly at first
    • more duality and internal conflict to his personality

Act 3

Scene 1

  • stupid musicians and clown make some comedy
  • Cassio immediately goes to Emilia to get Desdemona to talk to Cassio, Iago promises to get Othello out of the way

Scene 2

  • super short, basically the point is that Othello gonna look at the fortifications, shows Othello is in charge and doing his jobby job
  • he’s the most powerful figure while also being plenty distracted
    • hes at his highest point in power, he can only fall, he also has only two confidants in Iago and Desdemona, Iago is obviously trying to become the only one

Scene 3

  • starts off with Desdemona being quite convinced in Cassio’s favor
    • like really convinced
    • like convinced exactly as much as Iago thought
  • Othello and Iago walk in and Cassio immediately bounces
  • Othello notices but Iago kinds half denies it to convince him more, Desdemona confirms it and immediately tries to convince him
    • man shes really tries hard at convincing him
    • like oh my god geez lady do you want to imply yourself in an affair?
    • also first interaction as a married couple we see an its all about Cassio and Desdemona is being annoying
  • Othello keeps asking Iago what he knows and Iago is intentionally playing very coy
    • Iago is slowly going to break down Othello by making him unsure
    • Othello is like you aren’t my friend if you don’t explain whats going on
    • Iago confesses that he is jealous and is often suspicious
      • its better if I don’t tell you what I know because its not good
  • Othello will need proof to be convinced
    • call back to 1.3 because Iago uses that she elopes without he father’s consent to help convince Othello
  • Othello has a little soliloquy where me turns into Iago
    • Othello starts to show some self loathing
    • starts to lose his confidence
    • questions he race, his speaking ability, and his age

    • “My relief must be to loath her” (309-310)

    • from this point on he is doomed, its a downwards spiral
  • Desdemona gives Othello handkerchief, Othello throws it away
    • the handkerchief is a symbol of their love and its now fallen into the hands of Iago
      • one he gives them the very proof they need to confirm his bias
      • secondly he throws away their love essentially
    • Emilia picks it up and gives it to Iago who will undoubtedly use it as proof of Cassio and Desdemona’s affair
  • Othello is now regretting that he knows what Iago told him because its bugging him
  • Iago says Cassio has been dreaming of Desdemona in her sleep
  • Othello starts getting really angry
    • Iago tries to discourage him possibly for credibility or some reverse psychology
    • Othello says he will never change his mind
      • he’s lost it
  • Othello orders Iago to killCassio
    • 3 days
  • Othello goes to go plan how to kill Desdemona
  • he promotes Iago to lieutenant (Iago’s Plan to Destroy Othello)

Scene 4

  • starts off with Desdemona looking for Cassio with the clowns
  • Desdemona doesn’t know where her handkerchief is but itsn’t too worried because she believes Othello isn’t jealous
  • Othello roles up
    • so the thought at the time was that moist hands meant that you were ill
      • Desdemona has moist hands
    • it also supposedly indicates that she was looking for and affair essentially
    • Othello’s language also completely changes from chaste to using sexual innuendos like Iago
  • Sneaky sneaky Othello asks to use her handkerchief
    • makes up this bullcrap about how important it was as a family heirloom
    • trying to make her feel guilty
    • unfortunately Desdemona tries to bring up Cassio and they have a back and forth where Othello wants the handkerchief and Desdemona wants to talk about Cassio
  • Desdemona is confused why Othello is so different now
    • assumes that Othello is upset because something about war has gone wrong or politics or something
    • Emilia suspects jealousy
  • Oh dang Bianca shows up
    • now this is funny because we see Bianca and Cassio in a cute little relationship
    • Cassio gives Bianca Desdemona’s handkerchief
      • Bianca thinks that its from another woman
      • she’s been infected with the jealousy

Act 4

Scene 1

  • starts off with Othello and Iago talking about Desdemona and Cassio in bed with each other
  • Othello starts losing it
    • basically has a seizure
  • Um wow some stuff i forgot to write down
  • Then Iago and Othello plot to kill Desdemona by strangulation
    • will seal the deal on Othello’s broken mind
  • Lodovico roles up with a message
    • Desdemona tries to get him to help her with the Cassio problem
      • come on girl
  • Lodovico is confused why Othello is being such a meany
    • “is he stupid?”
    • Iago merely tells Lodovico to wait and see

Scene 2

  • Othello talking to Emilia and asking her about Desdemona and Cassio
    • Emilia says that Desdemona is honest
  • Othello starts making Desdemona swear she is honest upon heaven, this makes her double damned, by him and by God
    • Iago level craftiness here
  • Othello biggy mean
    • gives Desdemona some money cause she’s a whore and then leaves
  • Desdemona confused and unfortunately blaming herself basically
    • makes a vow

    • ” and his unkindness may defeat my life. / But never taint my love” (189-190)

  • Roderigo shows up
    • reveals that Iago has been telling Roderigo to give him jewels and stuff for Desdemona but Iago has just been pocketing them
    • Roderigo threatens to basically ruin Iago’s entire plan
    • Iago buys himself a bit more time
    • Iago manipulates Roderigo to kill Cassio so that Othello and Desdemona will stay for a funeral
      • Iago’s Plan to Destroy Othello

Scene 3

  • Othello makes Desdemona fire Emilia
  • Desdemona sings creepy and sad song
  • uh they have a conversation about cheating
    • Desdemona can’t imagine it
    • Emilia kinda blames the husband’s because they do it to
      • also she’d do it for more power

Act 5

Scene 1

  • Iago tells Roderigo to stab Cassio
  • In an aside Iago says he wants both to die
    • so Roderigo doesn’t get his stolen money back
    • and Cassio to die so he doesn’t make Iago feel bad because Cassio is a better person
  • Roderigo falls and Iago stabs Cassio in the leg
  • Iago sneaks away and then comes back and stabs Roderigo
    • tricks Lodovico and Gratiano
  • Iago blames Bianca for the attack
  • Iago sends Emilia to go inform Othello and Desdemona of the attack
    • partly wants Emilia to see Othello kill Desdemona
    • also this tells Othello that Iago succeeded

Scene 2

  • Othello talking about Desdemona’s beauty, he creeping on her while she’s sleeping
    • if he puts out his light (to kill her in darkness) he extinguishes his reason and her life
  • Othello tells Desdemona to repent so that she will go to heaven cause he’s about to kill her
    • Desdemona says her only sin was loving Othello too much dayyyyyuuuuummmm
  • Othello smothers Desdemona
  • Emilia comes in and informs Othello of the murder of Roderigo and the injury of Cassio
  • Desdemona pops back up as if from the dead and blames herself for her death
  • Emilia then realizes that Iago caused all of this and then calls out for murder
    • Emilia then asks Iago to tell the truth
    • Iago says that he just told him some information and confirmed his biases
  • Emilia tells all
  • Othello realizes hes been played and charges Iago
  • Iago kills Emilia
  • Othello then stabs Iago a bit later but does not kill
  • Othello stabs himself
    • wants to be known as

      • “one that loved not wisely, but too well” (404)

    • he was supposed to be tried, but instead because of his hubris he decides to mete out his own justice
  • Cassio inherits all of Othello’s stuff and becomes governor
  • Iago looks upon his “masterpiece” which is Desdemona, Emilia, and Othello lying dead on a bed

Bianca

  • A woman in Cyprus in love with Cassio

Brabantio

  • a Venetian senator
  • father of Desdemona
  • kinda super racist ngl
  • dies from sadness like a loser

Cassio

  • Othello’s second in command
  • Promoted to lieutenant over Iago
  • Very gentlemanly and polite around women

Cyprus

  • Setting of Act 2 on
  • attacked by the Turks which is why the cast ends up here

Desdemona

  • a Venetian lady
  • wife to Othello
  • literally just a really good person, this is something Iago eventually will leverage against her and Othello in Act 3

Duke

  • Duke of Venice
    • head honcho

Emilia

  • Iago’s wife

Gratiano

  • another gentleman

Iago

  • Othello’s standard-bearer, or “ancient”
  • friend of Othello before he tries to destroy him and stuff
  • hates Cassio because of his promotion
  • Doesn’t really respect marriage as seen in 2.1 ^39ebb8

Iago’s Plan to Destroy Othello

Motivations

  • Ostensibly that he wanted the promotion but Cassio was picked over him ^df44bc
  • Also because there’s a rumor that Othello slept with his wife and he wants to do the same basically
    • He acknowledges and is aware that this is likely untrue
  • Wants to make Othello extremely jealous…more like Iago
    • if he can make Othello like himself and himself more like Othello he’ll love himself more

Le Plot

  1. Make Roderigo his ally (1.1)
  2. Turns Brabantio against Othello (1.1)
  3. Tells Roderigo to “put money in thy purse” (1.3)
  4. Convinces Roderigo that Cassio and Desdemona are having an affair (2.1)
  5. Tells Roderigo to provoke Cassio into a fight 2.1
  6. Persuades Cassio to talk to Desdemona to get his job back (2.3)
  7. Will tell Emilia to tell Desdemona to look out for Cassio (2.3)
  8. Will ensure Othello witnesses Cassio & Desdemona talking alone (2.3)
  9. Has Emilia steal Desdemona’s handkerchief to plant in Cassio’s room (3.3)
  10. Tells Othello a lie that he overheard Cassio having a spicy dream about Desdemona (3.3)
  11. Entices Cassio to talk about Bianca with Othello nearby who believes hes talking about Desdemona (4.1)
  12. Plots with Othello to murder Desdemona and Cassio (4.1)
  13. Convinces Lodovico that Othello has become unstable 4.1
  14. Tells Roderigo that Othello & Desdemona are going to Mauritania (NW Africa) so the only way to keep Desdemona nearby is for him to kill Cassio so Othello will have to remain in Cyprus as Governor (4.2) ^ef9763

Montano

Othello

  • husband to Desdemona
  • Superior of Iago
  • of royal descent (revealed in 1.2)
  • Incredibly self-confident, greatest strength yet ultimate downfall

Roderigo

  • friend of Iago
  • loves Desdemona

Venice

  • The city that Othello is loyal too
  • ruled by the Duke
